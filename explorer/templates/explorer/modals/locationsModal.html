Geography of the JHB

Generally speaking, geocoding is the process of placing objects on a map. Geocoding texts usually involves some form of Named Entity Recognition (identifying passages that refer to locations), and then the engagement of a geolocation service that matches those phrases with known geographic features. But like many techniques that are computationally easy to perform, the results of such an automated procedure can be difficult or impossible to interpret. Using NER and geolocation on academic texts usually generates numerous spurious results: personal names are often confused with place names, and bylines and bibliographic citations pollute the results (e.g. "Kluwer Academic Publishers, Dordrecht, the Netherlands.")
