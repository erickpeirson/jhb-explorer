Organisms in {{ document.title }}

The taxonomic names listed in the Organisms panel at the bottom of this page represent taxa to which were likely referred in the article {{ document.title }}. This list was generated using a technique called Named Entity Recognition (NER).

NER is a machine learning technique for detecting references to classes of concepts, or entities, in texts. NER models typically focus on detecting references to people, organizations, and places, but can also find things like currency, quantities, and organisms. NER isn't fool-proof: the quality of the results depend both on the suitability of the NER model and on the quality of the texts.

We used an NER model called LINNAEUS to find references to taxa in JHB articles. LINNAEUS was trained on abstracts in the PubMed database, and is unusual among NER models in that it also tries to disambiguate the entities that it finds. In other words, it identifies not merely that a passage refers to an organism, but also which specific organism is being discussed. LINNAEUS' vocabulary of organisms is the NCBI Taxonomy database, which means that we can retrieve additional information about each organism discussed in JHB using the NCBI Entrez APIs.

Click on a taxon name to see more information about a taxon, including a list of other articles in which that taxon occurs, as well as external resources like gene sequences, datasets, and phylogenetic information.

Methodological Notes
